## 🔮 EMERGING BUZZ (From VC Land)

**Voice AI (Score: 32.0)**  
*Sources: Sequoia + 4 YC companies*

This isn't your grandmother's Alexa anymore. The chatter I'm seeing suggests we're talking about AI that can handle complex, multi-turn conversations – think enterprise phone systems that can actually *solve* customer problems instead of just routing them to death.

**Mike's take:** Finally. We've been waiting for voice interfaces that don't make you want to throw your phone. The enterprise applications here are massive – customer service, sales qualification, internal helpdesks. But here's the thing: "voice AI" is too generic for premium domains. 

**Domain patterns to watch:** Look for compound constructions like "VoiceIntelligence[something].com" or "[Industry]VoiceAI.com". The real money will be in vertical-specific applications: "LegalVoiceAI", "FinanceVoiceAI", "HealthcareVoiceAI". Generic is dead; specific is where the exits happen.

**Build AI (Score: 31.5)**  
*Sources: Sequoia + 3 YC companies*

This is interesting because it suggests democratization – platforms that let non-technical teams assemble AI workflows. Think Zapier meets AI model orchestration.

**Mike's take:** I'm bullish on this one. Every Fortune 500 I consult with has the same problem: their business units want AI capabilities, but IT can't keep up with demand. "Build AI" platforms solve the bottleneck. This screams enterprise SaaS opportunity.

**Domain implications:** "Build" compounds are gold right now. "BuildAI[Function].com", "Enterprise[Build/Builder]AI.com", "[Industry]AIBuilder.com". The pattern that's working is Action + AI + Specificity.

**Embodied Intelligence (Score: 30.5)**  
*Sources: Sequoia + 1 YC company*

Now we're getting spicy. This is AI that lives in physical robots – not just chatbots, but systems that can manipulate the real world. Think robotic process automation, but literally with robots.

**Mike's take:** This is 2-3 years out from mainstream enterprise adoption, but when Sequoia starts using terms like this, you pay attention. The applications in manufacturing, logistics, and healthcare are obvious. Less obvious: the massive infrastructure needed to support it.

**Domain patterns:** "Embodied" is clunky for domains, but "PhysicalAI", "RobotIntelligence", "EmbodiedAI" could work. More interesting: the infrastructure plays. "RobotCloud", "PhysicalAIOS", "EmbodiedPlatform". Think picks-and-shovels naming.

**AI Agent (Score: 31.0)**  
*Sources: Sequoia + 2 YC companies*

The singular vs. plural here is telling. "AI agents" (plural) is already crossing to mainstream, but "AI agent" (singular) suggests they're talking about specific, focused implementations.

**Mike's take:** This is where the enterprise money is. Not general AI assistants, but specialized agents that handle specific business functions. Think "procurement agent", "compliance agent", "customer onboarding agent".

Pattern recognition time: Sequoia is pushing hard on practical AI applications. Not AGI, not sci-fi – practical business solutions. The keyword clustering around "build", "implement", "agent", and "platform" tells me they're funding the picks-and-shovels layer.

The naming pattern that wins: [Enterprise Function] + AI + [Action/Platform]. Think "FinanceAIOperations", "HRAIPlatform", "ComplianceAIBuilder".
